<p><p>The micro lithium-ion battery market is categorized into two main types: rechargeable and non-rechargeable batteries. Rechargeable micro lithium-ion batteries are designed for multiple charge-discharge cycles, making them ideal for applications such as smartphones, wearables, and portable electronics, where longevity and efficiency are essential. Conversely, non-rechargeable micro lithium-ion batteries provide a one-time use solution, typically found in devices requiring low power and infrequent battery changes, such as remote controls and certain medical devices. Both types serve distinct market needs.</p></p>
