<p><ul><li>Pulse Type</li><li>Continuous Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Solid lasers are categorized into two primary types based on their operation modes: pulse type and continuous wave (CW) type. Pulse-type solid lasers emit energy in short, concentrated bursts, making them ideal for applications requiring high peak power, such as material processing and medical procedures. In contrast, continuous wave solid lasers produce a constant beam of light, suitable for applications like laser cutting, engraving, and telecommunications, where sustained power output is crucial. Both types have unique advantages tailored to specific industrial needs.</p></p>
