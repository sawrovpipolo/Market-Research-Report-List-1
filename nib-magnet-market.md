<p><p>The NIB (Neodymium-Iron-Boron) magnet market can be categorized into two primary types based on the manufacturing processes: bonding and sintering. Bonding involves combining powdered magnet materials with a polymer resin, resulting in flexible and intricately shaped magnets suitable for various applications. Sintering, on the other hand, involves compacting and heating powdered materials at high temperatures to form dense, robust magnets with superior magnetic properties. Both methods cater to distinct market needs, influencing performance, cost, and application suitability.</p></p>
