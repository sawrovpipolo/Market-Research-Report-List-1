<p><p>Lithium extraction from salt lake brine involves various market methods, each with unique processes. The precipitation method uses chemical reactions to form solid lithium compounds. The adsorption method employs materials to capture lithium ions from brine. Electrodialysis utilizes electric fields to separate lithium through ion-selective membranes. Membrane separation relies on selective permeability for ion separation. The calcination method involves heating to decompose lithium compounds, while solvent extraction employs solvents to selectively dissolve and separate lithium, optimizing recovery efficiency.</p></p>
