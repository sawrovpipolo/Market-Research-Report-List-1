<p><p>The Low Power Audio Codecs market can be categorized into two main types: analog and digital. Analog codecs process audio signals in their natural form, often resulting in higher fidelity but with greater power consumption. In contrast, digital codecs convert audio signals into digital formats, enabling more efficient data compression and lower power usage, making them ideal for battery-operated devices. Both types serve distinct applications, with digital codecs dominating in consumer electronics, while analog codecs are often found in high-end audio equipment.</p></p>
