<p><p>The implant-borne prosthetics market is primarily categorized into bone level and tissue level prosthetics. Bone level prosthetics involve implants that are placed below the gum line, providing a stable foundation for crowns or bridges, typically offering excellent stability and aesthetic results. In contrast, tissue level prosthetics are positioned above the bone, making them easier to place and maintain. Both types cater to different patient needs and anatomical conditions, driving advancements in design and materials for improved outcomes in dental restoration.</p></p>
