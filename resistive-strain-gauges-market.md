<p><p>The resistive strain gauge market is primarily categorized into digital and pointer types. Digital strain gauges provide precise, numerical readings of strain, often incorporating advanced features like data logging and connectivity options. They are favored for applications requiring high accuracy and easy data interpretation. In contrast, pointer strain gauges use analog displays, offering a visual representation of strain changes. These are typically simpler and more cost-effective, suitable for straightforward installations or applications where digital technology is unnecessary.</p></p>
